Introduction

 A monitor is a computer screen, probably the most important computer device, because it is the main interface output device which shows data to the user. It can also be output/input if it's touchscreen.

LCD (liquid-crystal display)) is a flat screen made by a number of color or monochromes pixels located in front of a light source or reflector

A cathode-ray tube (CRT) is a technology that enables watching images thorugh a beam of cathodic lights that's constantly directed against a glass screen covered with phosphorus and lead. It's very old-fashioned.

LED (light-emitting diode) monitors are characterized for being composed by LEDs. LEDs screens biggest advantage is having no size limit.

History

 The history of monitors is usually organized in 4 main generations:

1st generation

In the first computers, people communicated with them through little lights, which would turn on or off according to the memory positions the computer is accessing or the insructions the computer is running.

2nd generation

During the 50's, the most common way to interact with a computer is through a teletype, which connected directly to the computer and printed all the data from a computing sesion.

3rd generation

In the 70's, CRT monitors started, it's designed to mainly display text and supported underline, bold font, italics, among others... A bit later CGA monitors came out, which were comercialized in 1981 along with the development of the first graphic card.

 After learning about it's characteristics, in this section you will learn what type to monitor to buy according to the characteristics you want:

Type of panel

 Commercially, monitos are distinguished by these 3 types of panels:
IPS

It's the best type of panel because offers the best colors, however they are the most expensive and usually have high latency and low refresh rate.

TN

It's the cheapest and offers the worst color quality of the 3, however it has the lowest latency and higher refresh rate usually.

VA

It's in an intermediate point between the other 2

Refresh rate

 For a common use, 60hz is enough, but for gaming it's recommendable 120 or 144hz sometimes, more than that is unnecesary

Size and format

 The only recommendable format nowadays is 16:9, because 4:3 is obsolte and wider monitors are way too expensive.
The most common sizes are 24 and 27 inches.
